About Me

I am currently an Assistant Professor in the Department of Economics at Diponegoro University, Indonesia. My academic journey led me to complete my PhD in Economics at the prestigious University of Muenster, Germany in 2019.

My research interests lie at the intersection of Political Economics and Economic Development, where I explore how institutional changes shape economic outcomes and political behavior.

Research Focus

My recent work explores the political economy of decentralization, investigating how the adoption of decentralization policies fundamentally rearranges the incentive structures faced by political agents.

I am particularly interested in understanding how these institutional changes determine both political and economic outcomes, contributing to our broader understanding of governance and development processes.
