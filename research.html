Research

Exploring the intersection of political economy, decentralization, and economic development through rigorous empirical analysis and innovative methodological approaches.

Research Philosophy

My research program centers on understanding how political institutions and governance structures influence economic outcomes, particularly in developing countries. I employ a multidisciplinary approach that combines economic theory, political science insights, and advanced econometric methods to address fundamental questions about decentralization, institutional quality, and regional development.

Through both theoretical modeling and empirical analysis, I seek to provide evidence-based insights that can inform policy decisions and contribute to our understanding of how to design institutions that promote sustainable economic growth and improved welfare outcomes.

Research Methodology

My research employs a diverse set of methodological approaches, combining theoretical rigor with empirical innovation to address complex questions in political economy and development.

Causal Identification

Utilizing natural experiments, instrumental variables, regression discontinuity, and difference-in-differences designs to establish causal relationships in observational data.

Panel Data Analysis

Leveraging longitudinal data structures to control for unobserved heterogeneity and track dynamic processes over time using fixed effects and random effects models.

Spatial Econometrics

Incorporating spatial dependencies and spillover effects in economic analysis through spatial lag, spatial error, and spatial Durbin models.

Machine Learning

Applying machine learning techniques for prediction, classification, and pattern recognition in large datasets, including ensemble methods and deep learning approaches.

Field Experiments

Designing and implementing randomized controlled trials to test theoretical predictions and evaluate policy interventions in real-world settings.

Mixed Methods

Combining quantitative analysis with qualitative insights through surveys, interviews, and case studies to provide comprehensive understanding of complex phenomena.

Research Impact & Policy Relevance

My research aims to bridge the gap between academic scholarship and practical policy applications. Through collaboration with government agencies, international organizations, and civil society groups, I strive to ensure that research findings contribute to evidence-based policymaking and improved development outcomes.

Key policy areas where my research has provided insights include: fiscal decentralization reforms, local government capacity building, electoral system design, and regional development strategies. I regularly present findings to policymakers and participate in policy advisory committees to maximize the real-world impact of academic research.
